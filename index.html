Avatars

  1. Matsya (The Fish): Rescues the sacred scriptures and sages from a great flood.

  2. Kurma (The Tortoise): Supports Mount Mandara to churn the ocean for nectar (amrita).

  3. Varaha (The Boar): Lifts the Earth out of the cosmic ocean. Narasimha

  4. (The Man-Lion): Defeats the demon king Hiranyakashipu, protecting his devotee Prahlada.

  5. Vamana (The Dwarf): Subdues the demon king Bali by taking three steps and reclaiming the heavens.

  6. Parashurama (The Warrior with an Axe): Destroys corrupt rulers and restores justice.

  7. Rama: The hero of the epic Ramayana, who rescues his wife Sita from the demon king Ravana.

  8. Krishna: A central figure in the Mahabharata and Bhagavad Gita, he plays a key role in guiding the Pandavas and is known for his divine teachings and miracles.

  9. Buddha: Some traditions include Gautama Buddha as an avatar of Vishnu, representing the spread of compassion and non-violence.

  10. Kalki (The Future Avatar): An anticipated future incarnation, who will appear at the end of the current age, Kali Yuga, to restore righteousness.
